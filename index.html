Full 8-Week Routine Details

Reference all exercises and weekly plans below:

Weeks 1-2: Foundation and Gentle Opening

General Schedule (Aim for 3-4 times a week):

  • Warm-up: 5-10 minutes
  • Stretches: 30 seconds hold, 2 repetitions each
  • Strengthening: 10 repetitions, 2 sets each
  • Cool-down

Exercises:

  • Stretches: Butterfly, Figure Four, Low Lunge.
  • Strengthening: Clamshells, Glute Bridges.
  • Sitting Practice: 1-2 minutes, several times a day. Use props (cushions under hips and/or knees). Focus on long spine.
Detailed Exercise Descriptions
Butterfly Stretch (Baddha Konasana):

Sit on the floor, bring the soles of your feet together, and let your knees fall out to the sides. Hold your feet with your hands. Gently press your knees towards the floor, either with your hands or by leaning forward from your hips (keeping your back straight). Focus: Inner thighs (adductors) and hip external rotation.

Figure Four Stretch / Supine Pigeon Pose:

Lie on your back with knees bent, feet flat on the floor. Cross your right ankle over your left knee. Reach through the gap and grab the back of your left thigh (or shin if you can reach) and gently pull your left knee towards your chest. Keep your right foot flexed to protect your knee. Repeat on the other side. Focus: Hip external rotation (targeting glutes and piriformis).

Low Lunge (Anjaneyasana):

Start in a kneeling position. Step your right foot forward between your hands, so your knee is directly above your ankle. Slide your left knee back until you feel a stretch in the front of your left hip (hip flexor). Keep your spine long. You can place your hands on your front knee, on the floor, or raise them overhead. Repeat on the other side. Focus: Hip flexor flexibility.

Clamshells:

Lie on your side, knees bent at a 90-degree angle, and feet stacked. Keep your feet together and lift your top knee towards the ceiling. Slowly lower it back down. Focus: Hip abductors and external rotators (gluteus medius).

Glute Bridges:

Lie on your back with knees bent, feet flat on the floor, hip-width apart. Lift your hips off the floor until your body forms a straight line from shoulders to knees. Squeeze your glutes at the top. Slowly lower back down. Focus: Glutes and core.

Weeks 3-4: Gradual Deepening

  • Stretches: Butterfly (begin gently leaning forward), Figure Four (gentle pull), Low Lunge (hold longer). Introduce Seated Forward Fold.
  • Strengthening: Continue Clamshells, Glute Bridges. Focus on controlled movements.
  • Sitting Practice: 3-5 minutes. Start to notice where you feel the stretch in the cross-legged position.
Detailed Exercise Descriptions (New Exercises)
Seated Forward Fold (Paschimottanasana):

Sit with legs extended straight out in front of you. Hinge from your hips, reaching forward towards your feet. Keep your spine long, avoid rounding your back excessively. Focus: Hamstrings and lower back, which can indirectly impact hip mobility.

Weeks 5-6: Targeted Flexibility and Active Engagement

  • Stretches: Butterfly (deeper forward fold, consider gentle knee presses), Figure Four (deeper pull). *Cautiously* introduce Frog Pose (very gentle, minimal width, stay on hands if forearms too intense).
  • Strengthening: Continue Clamshells (consider a light resistance band), Glute Bridges. Introduce Side-Lying Leg Lifts.
  • Sitting Practice: 5-8 minutes. Experiment with bringing heels slightly closer to your groin if comfortable.
Detailed Exercise Descriptions (New Exercises)
Frog Pose (Mandukasana - Advanced):

Start on all fours. Slowly widen your knees, keeping your ankles in line with your knees (feet pointing outwards). Lower onto your forearms, keeping your hips in line with your knees. This is a very intense inner thigh stretch. Go slowly! Focus: Deep inner thigh (adductor) stretch.

Side-Lying Leg Lifts:

Lie on your side, legs stacked and straight. Lift your top leg straight up towards the ceiling, leading with your heel. Slowly lower back down. Focus: Hip abductors.

Weeks 7-8: Consolidation and Maintenance Preparation

  • Stretches: Continue all previous stretches, aiming for your comfortable maximum. Increase hold times to 45-60 seconds, 3 repetitions.
  • Strengthening: Maintain all strengthening exercises, potentially increasing repetitions or sets.
  • Sitting Practice: 8-15+ minutes. Gradually try reducing props under your knees, observing if your knees remain lower. Continue using hip elevation as needed for spinal comfort.

Tips for Success

  • Consistency over Intensity: A little bit often is much better than a lot once in a while.
  • Mind-Body Connection: Pay attention to how your body feels. Breathe into the stretches.
  • Use Props:
    • Cushions/Folded Blankets under hips: Elevating your hips helps to tilt your pelvis forward, making it easier to maintain a straight spine and allowing your knees to drop lower. This is crucial!
    • Cushions/Blocks under knees: If your knees are very high, placing cushions or blocks under them provides support and can help release tension, preventing overstretching of the inner thighs initially.
  • Everyday Movement: Incorporate more squatting, lunging, and hip-opening movements into your daily life.
  • Stay Hydrated: Good hydration helps with tissue elasticity.
  • Be Patient: Flexibility gains are slow. Celebrate small improvements. You might notice your knees dropping a few degrees, or you can sit for longer without discomfort.
